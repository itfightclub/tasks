# Important Legal Notice 

The developers of this software assert that requiring age attestation or 
verification constitutes an unreasonable burden on the Open Source Community. 
This software will not comply with such requirements and is expressly 
prohibited for use in any jurisdiction that mandates them.

The imposition of age verification is viewed as an unreasonable search and 
seizure of Personally Identifiable Information (PII). This software 
categorically refuses to engage in practices deemed absurd, particularly in
light of the recurrent headlines regarding data breaches and security 
vulnerabilities that compromise individuals' PII. It is increasingly 
inappropriate to legislate against the protection of private information.

## For Individuals Affected by these Regulations

Individuals who are impacted by these laws, including any future legislation,
are hereby prohibited from using this software due to governmental decisions.
It is advisable to contact your local representatives and leaders to express 
your concerns regarding these laws in a respectful and constructive manner.

For reference materials, please see:

* [csa-scientist-open-letter](https://csa-scientist-open-letter.org/ageverif-Feb2026)

## For Government Entities Enforcing Such Regulations

Due to actions taken by your governing body, you are similarly prohibited from
using this software, effective on the same date that your residents are 
restricted from its use. It is unlawful for you to download, utilize, execute, 
install, compile, copy, or distribute this software or its source code. Each 
breach of this agreement shall result in a non-dischargeable debt.

Penalties:

* California government: $15,000 (2 * $7,500) per violation
* Colorado government: $15,000 (2 * $7,500) per violation

Any government entity not explicitly mentioned that enforces age attestation or
verification for Open Source software, Operating Systems, or application stores 
is also barred from using this software. Violations will be subject to 
injunctive relief and penalties that are double the amounts specified by
your applicable laws.

## Notice to Any Future Government Entity Seeking to Limit Open Source

Should you enact legislation similar to those outlined above, thereby denying 
your citizens the right to access free and open-source software, you 
concurrently relinquish your right to utilize this software and will incur 
twice the fines applicable to your constituents.

Please examine the definition of [idiocracy][idiot], as a significant portion
of contemporary society relies on Open Source technology, including many 
systems operated under your jurisdiction. You are benefiting from the 
foundational work of countless innovators yet are employing these freely 
available resources to impose restrictions upon the very individuals who 
contributed to their development.
